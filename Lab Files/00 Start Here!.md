# Welcome! 

Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models are transforming how we learn, work, and solve problems in cybersecurity. This hands-on workshop introduces prompt engineering fundamentals specifically designed for students entering the cybersecurity field.

Whether you're studying for certifications, trying to understand complex technical concepts, or building your skill set, knowing how to effectively communicate with AI tools can dramatically accelerate your learning journey. This workshop will teach you how to craft prompts that break down difficult topics, generate study materials, and fill knowledge gaps as you progress in your cybersecurity education.

This course was designed for a three-hour workshop intended to provide practical experience with tools and resources necessary for working with common "frontier" LLMs like ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini, and Perplexity. You'll move from basic conversational prompts to creating a personalized library of reusable prompts tailored to your learning needs.

We'll also address the cybersecurity perspective on these tools, discussing prompt injection, adversarial attacks on LLMs, and why understanding these vulnerabilities matters in your future career.

**What You'll Learn:**

- How AI and Large Language Models work and where they fit in the tech landscape
- Fundamentals of effective prompt engineering
- Hands-on experience with LLMs like ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini, and Perplexity
- Techniques for using AI to explain complex concepts, create study materials, and accelerate learning
- Introduction to prompt injection and LLM security considerations
- How to build and maintain your own prompt library for ongoing studies

**What You'll Build:** By the end of this workshop, you'll have created a personalized prompt library containing 5-6 tested, reusable prompts designed to support your cybersecurity learning journey.

**Who Should Attend:** This workshop is designed for students new to cybersecurity with beginner-level foundational knowledge. No prior experience with AI tools or prompt engineering required.

**Prerequisites:**

- Basic familiarity with cybersecurity concepts
- Comfort using web applications and basic computer navigation
- No prior AI or prompt engineering experience needed

**Technical Requirements:**

- Laptop with internet connectivity
- ChatGPT, Claude, or Copilot account (free tier acceptable)
- Text editor or note-taking application


**Format:** Highly interactive with three hands-on lab sessions. Bring a laptop and be ready to experiment, learn, and share with your peers.
